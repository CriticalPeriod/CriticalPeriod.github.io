Professional summary

After the first cycle of medical studies, I integrated the highly selective MD-PhD program of the Ecole Normale Superieure and the lab of Alain Prochiantz to work on the molecular mechanisms involved in brain structural plasticity, with an emphasis on critical periods in development. We developed two models of neurological disorders, and studied the effect of adult brain plasticity enhancing as a potential therapeutic strategy. We also provided evidence for the lack of specificity of plasticity pathways in different types of functional plasticity paradigms. I developed new techniques myself in the lab, including CUT&RUN, ATAC-Seq, TRAP, and their respective data analysis, as well as behavioral testing and associated video analysis using machine learning that are still used by the lab's students. I am now directed towards artificial intelligence, with the long-term goal of working at the interface of medicine, neurosciences and robotics. My latest project includes the use of NengoDL to model Spiking neural network. I would like to implement brain-derived principles in algorithms.
