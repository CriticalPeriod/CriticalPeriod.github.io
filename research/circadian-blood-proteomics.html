Inferring organ circadian rhythms from blood proteomics

2025 · Stanford · Mignot Lab · Benacom D. et al.

Summary

Circadian rhythms govern virtually every organ system, yet measuring organ-specific clocks in humans is challenging. In this project at Emmanuel Mignot's lab at Stanford, we apply machine learning to blood protein data to infer organ-related circadian signals from peripheral blood markers alone.

Our approach uses proteomic time-series data to deconvolve organ-specific circadian components, potentially enabling non-invasive assessment of internal clock function. This work bridges chronobiology and clinical diagnostics, with implications for personalized medicine and sleep disorders.

Key findings

  • Blood proteins carry organ-specific circadian signatures
  • Machine learning can deconvolve organ-level signals from peripheral blood
  • Potential for non-invasive circadian health assessment
