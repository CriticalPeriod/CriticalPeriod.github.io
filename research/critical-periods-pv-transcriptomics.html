PV interneuron transcriptomics across plasticity paradigms

2024 · Journal Placeholder · Benacom D. et al.

Summary

Parvalbumin-positive (PV) interneurons are key regulators of critical period plasticity in the developing brain. In this study, we performed transcriptomic profiling of PV cells across multiple enhanced-plasticity paradigms to identify shared molecular drivers of plasticity reopening.

Surprisingly, PV cells did not converge on a single common transcriptomic pathway. Instead, our data suggest that multiple, partially independent genetic routes can lead to similar functional plasticity outcomes. This finding challenges the notion of a unified molecular switch for critical period regulation and opens new avenues for combinatorial therapeutic strategies.

Key findings

  • PV interneurons lack a single shared transcriptomic program across plasticity paradigms
  • Multiple genetic pathways converge on similar functional plasticity
  • Methods established: CUT&RUN, ATAC-seq, and TRAP-seq analysis pipelines
